About Me

I graduated from the University of Illinois with a Computer Science major, and a minor in Game Studies and Design. I’m interested in pursuing a career in Computer Science related to education, either teaching Computer Science or teaching other subjects using my CS skills. I've also gained an interest in creating games over the years, especially in relation to how they can be used to inform and educate people in engaging ways.

Over the course of my college career, I've learned many principles on how to design engaging and fun games while keeping the target audience in mind. I've also gained a lot of experience in the area of prototyping, playtesting, and revising games. This has given me a good understanding of the challenges of designing games from start to finish.

I grew up in Champaign IL. I enjoy crafts like crochet and making jewelry. I’m involved in InterVarsity Christian Fellowship on campus. I’m creative, and I enjoy coming up with innovative solutions to problems. I decided to join the Leadership Certificate program because I felt I already had some of the basic skills needed to be a leader, and I wanted concrete training on what it meant to be a leader. I hoped to gain knowledge about specific skills I could practice throughout my college career.
